This grammar cheat sheet focuses on the essential rules and common usage patterns for Brazilian Portuguese.

### Sentence Structure and Basics

* **Word Order:** Brazilian Portuguese typically follows a **Subject + Verb + Object** (SVO) structure.


* **Questions:** Questions can be formed by adding a question mark and using a rising intonation, or by starting with interrogative words like *Quem* (Who), *O que* (What), *Onde* (Where), *Quando* (When), *Por quê* (Why), and *Como* (How).

### Nouns, Articles, and Gender

* **Gender:** All nouns are either masculine (usually ending in **-o**) or feminine (usually ending in **-a**). Exceptions exist, such as *o dia* (the day) and *a mão* (the hand).
* **Articles:** These must agree in gender and number with the noun.
* **Definite:** *o, os* (masc.); *a, as* (fem.).
* **Indefinite:** *um, uns* (masc.); *uma, umas* (fem.).


* **Plurals:** Generally formed by adding **-s** to vowels, replacing **-m** with **-ns** (*homem* → *homens*), or adding **-es** to nouns ending in **-r** or **-z** (*flor* → *flores*).

### Essential Pronouns

* **Subject Pronouns:** Brazilian Portuguese predominantly uses *você* (singular) and *vocês* (plural) for "you". Formal addresses use *o senhor* (Sir) or *a senhora* (Ma'am).
* **The "We" Rule:** In colloquial speech, ***a gente*** is frequently used instead of *nós* for "we." While it means "us," it always takes the **third-person singular** verb conjugation (*a gente vai* = we go).
* **Object Pronouns:** In casual Brazilian speech, object pronouns (me, te, o, a) usually come **before** the verb (*Eu te amo*). Informally, subject pronouns like *ele* or *ela* are often used as objects after the verb (*Eu vi ele* instead of the formal *Eu o vi*).

### Verb Conjugation (Present Indicative)

Regular verbs are categorized by their infinitive endings: **-ar**, **-er**, and **-ir**.

| Subject | -AR (e.g., *falar*) | -ER (e.g., *correr*) | -IR (e.g., *partir*) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Eu** | fal**o** | corr**o** | part**o** |
| **Você/Ele/Ela** | fal**a** | corr**e** | part**e** |
| **Nós** | fal**amos** | corr**emos** | part**imos** |
| **Vocês/Eles/Elas** | fal**am** | corr**em** | part**em** |

### Mandatory Contractions

Portuguese requires contracting prepositions with articles and certain pronouns.

* **De** (of/from) + *o/a* = **do / da**.
* **Em** (in/on) + *o/a* = **no / na**.
* **Por** (by/through) + *o/a* = **pelo / pela**.
* **A** (to) + *a* (the) = **à** (this is known as *crase*).

### Comparisons

* **Superiority:** *mais* + adjective + *(do) que* (e.g., *mais alto que*).
* **Equality:** *tão* + adjective + *quanto/como* (e.g., *tão inteligente quanto*).
* **Irregular Forms:** *bom* (good) → **melhor** (better); *mau* (bad) → **pior** (worse); *grande* (big) → **maior** (bigger); *pequeno* (small) → **menor** (smaller).
