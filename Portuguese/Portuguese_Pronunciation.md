This pronunciation cheat sheet covers the essential sounds of Brazilian Portuguese, which is known for its musicality and "open" vowel sounds compared to European Portuguese.

### Vowels: The Five Basic Sounds

Brazilian Portuguese has five written vowels, but their pronunciation changes depending on stress and position.

| Letter | Sound (English Approximation) | Examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **A** | **ah** as in "father" (Open) | *água*, *casa* |
| **E** | **eh** as in "bed" (Open) or **ay** as in "may" (Closed) | *pé* (open), *você* (closed) |
| **I** | **ee** as in "bee" | *filho*, *igreja* |
| **O** | **aw** as in "law" (Open) or **oh** as in "phone" (Closed) | *só* (open), *ovo* (closed) |
| **U** | **oo** as in "food" | *uva*, *rua* |

* **Terminal Vowel Trick:** At the very end of a word, an unstressed **-e** sounds like **"ee"** (*pode* → "poh-djee") and an unstressed **-o** sounds like **"oo"** (*carro* → "kah-hoo").

### The "D" and "T" Transformation

One of the most distinct features of the Brazilian accent is the softening of **D** and **T** when followed by the **"ee"** sound.

* **D + I/E:** Sounds like the **"dg"** in "dodge" or **"j"** in "jeep".
* *Dia* (day) sounds like "DJEE-ah".
* *Cidade* (city) sounds like "see-DAH-djee".


* **T + I/E:** Sounds like the **"ch"** in "cheese".
* *Noite* (night) sounds like "NOY-tchee".
* *Tia* (aunt) sounds like "TCHEE-ah".



### Consonants with Special Rules

* **H:** Always silent at the start of a word (*hora* → "oh-rah").
* **J:** Always sounds like the **"s"** in "pleasure" or "measure".
* **L at the end of a syllable:** Sounds like a **"u/oo"** (*Brasil* → "bra-SEE-oo"; *futebol* → "foo-tchee-BOH-oo").
* **R and RR:**
* **Single R (between vowels):** A light tongue tap, like the "tt" in "better" (*caro*).
* **RR or R (start of word):** A strong, raspy **"h"** sound like "hot" (*rio* → "HEE-oh"; *carro* → "KAH-hoo").


* **S:** Sounds like **"s"** at the start of a word, but like **"z"** when it sits between two vowels (*casa* → "kah-zah").
* **X (The Chameleon):** This letter can sound like **"sh"** (*xícara*), **"s"** (*texto*), **"z"** (*exame*), or **"ks"** (*táxi*).

### Digraphs (Letter Combinations)

* **LH:** Sounds like the **"ly"** in "million" (*olho* → "OH-lyoo").
* **NH:** Sounds like the **"ny"** in "onion" or the Spanish **ñ** (*sonho* → "SOH-nyoo").
* **CH:** Always sounds like the **"sh"** in "shop" (*chave* → "SHAH-vee").

### Nasal Sounds

Portuguese is famous for nasalization, often marked by a tilde (**~**) or a terminal **-m** or **-n**.

* **Ã / Ão:** Pronounced through the nose. **-ão** sounds roughly like a nasal "ow".
* **Terminal -m:** Do not close your lips. It simply turns the preceding vowel into a nasal sound (*bom* → "boh-ng").

### Word Stress

In Brazilian Portuguese, the "default" stress falls on the **penultimate (second-to-last) syllable**.

* Example: *ca-DEI-ra* (chair), *ja-NE-la* (window).
* If a word has an accent mark (á, ê, õ), that syllable is always the one you stress.
