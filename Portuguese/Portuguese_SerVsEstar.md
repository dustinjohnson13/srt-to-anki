## Ser vs. Estar: The Core Decision

Both mean "to be" in English, but they are not interchangeable.

**The one-sentence rule:**
- **Ser** = permanent or defining identity (who/what something *is*)
- **Estar** = temporary state or condition (how something *is right now*)

This rule has exceptions, but it covers ~80% of cases.

---

### SER — Use for:

| Category | Example | Translation |
|---|---|---|
| **Identity / name** | *Eu sou o Daniel.* | I am Daniel. |
| **Nationality / origin** | *Ela é brasileira.* | She is Brazilian. |
| **Profession** | *Ele é médico.* | He is a doctor. |
| **Personality traits** | *Você é inteligente.* | You are intelligent. |
| **Physical description (inherent)** | *Ela é alta.* | She is tall. |
| **Relationships** | *Ele é meu irmão.* | He is my brother. |
| **Material / composition** | *A mesa é de madeira.* | The table is made of wood. |
| **Time / dates / days** | *Hoje é segunda-feira.* | Today is Monday. |
| **Location of events** | *A festa é no meu apartamento.* | The party is at my apartment. |
| **Passive voice** | *O livro foi escrito por ela.* | The book was written by her. |

---

### ESTAR — Use for:

| Category | Example | Translation |
|---|---|---|
| **Emotions / feelings** | *Estou feliz hoje.* | I am happy today. |
| **Physical condition** | *Ele está doente.* | He is sick. |
| **Location of people/things** | *Ela está em casa.* | She is at home. |
| **Temporary states** | *A porta está aberta.* | The door is open. |
| **Progressive tenses** | *Estou comendo.* | I am eating. |
| **Result of a change** | *Estou casado.* | I am (now) married. |

---

### The Trap: Adjectives That Change Meaning

Some adjectives have different meanings depending on whether you use *ser* or *estar*. These are the ones that will trip you up most.

| Adjective | With SER | With ESTAR |
|---|---|---|
| **bonito/a** | is (inherently) beautiful | is looking beautiful right now |
| **vivo/a** | is lively (personality) | is alive |
| **morto/a** | *(rarely used with ser)* | is dead |
| **pronto/a** | is ready (by nature) | is ready (right now) |
| **seguro/a** | is safe (inherently secure) | is safe (currently out of danger) |
| **rico/a** | is rich (wealthy) | is rich/flavorful (food) |
| **mal / mau** | *Ele é mau* = He is evil | *Ele está mal* = He is unwell |

---

### Quick Decision Tree

```
Is it defining WHO or WHAT something fundamentally is?
  → SER

Is it describing HOW something is right now (state, location, feeling)?
  → ESTAR

Is it a location?
  → ESTAR for people and things
  → SER for events ("the concert is at the park")

Is it an emotion or physical condition?
  → Always ESTAR
```

### Conjugations at a Glance

| Subject | SER | ESTAR |
|---|---|---|
| Eu | sou | estou |
| Você / Ele / Ela | é | está |
| Nós | somos | estamos |
| Vocês / Eles / Elas | são | estão |

**Past (Preterite):**

| Subject | SER | ESTAR |
|---|---|---|
| Eu | fui | estive |
| Você / Ele / Ela | foi | esteve |
| Nós | fomos | estivemos |
| Vocês / Eles / Elas | foram | estiveram |

*Note: Ser and Ir share identical preterite forms. Context determines meaning.*
