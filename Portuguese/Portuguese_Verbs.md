This verb conjugation cheat sheet focuses on the most frequently used tenses in Brazilian Portuguese. Regular verbs follow predictable patterns based on their infinitive endings (**-ar**, **-er**, and **-ir**), while the most common verbs in the language are often irregular.

### Regular Verb Conjugations (Indicative Mood)

The indicative mood is used for factual statements.

| Tense | -AR (falar) | -ER (comer) | -IR (partir) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| **Present** | fal**o**, fal**a**, fal**amos**, fal**am** | com**o**, com**e**, com**emos**, com**em** | part**o**, part**e**, part**imos**, part**em** |
| **Preterite** (Simple Past) | fal**ei**, fal**ou**, fal**amos**, fal**aram** | com**i**, com**eu**, com**emos**, com**eram** | part**i**, part**iu**, part**imos**, part**iram** |
| **Imperfect** (Habitual Past) | fal**ava**, fal**ava**, fal**ávamos**, fal**avam** | com**ia**, com**ia**, com**íamos**, com**iam** | part**ia**, part**ia**, part**íamos**, part**iam** |
| **Future** (Immediate) | **vou** falar, **vai** falar, **vamos** falar, **vão** falar | **vou** comer, **vai** comer, **vamos** comer, **vão** comer | **vou** partir, **vai** partir, **vamos** partir, **vão** partir |

*Note: The forms listed correspond to **Eu**, **Você/Ele/Ela**, **Nós**, and **Vocês/Eles/Elas**.*

### Essential Irregular Verbs

These verbs are used constantly but do not follow regular patterns, particularly in the Preterite tense.

| Verb | Present (Eu, Você, Nós, Vocês) | Preterite (Eu, Você, Nós, Vocês) |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Ser** (to be) | sou, é, somos, são | fui, foi, fomos, foram |
| **Ir** (to go) | vou, vai, vamos, vão | fui, foi, fomos, foram |
| **Ter** (to have) | tenho, tem, temos, têm | tive, teve, tivemos, tiveram |
| **Estar** (to be) | estou, está, estamos, estão | estive, esteve, estivemos, estiveram |
| **Fazer** (to do/make) | faço, faz, fazemos, fazem | fiz, fez, fizemos, fizeram |
| **Poder** (can) | posso, pode, podemos, podem | pude, pôde, pudemos, puderam |

*Historical quirk: **Ser** and **Ir** share identical forms in the Preterite, Imperfect Subjunctive, and Future Subjunctive.*

### The Subjunctive Mood (Uncertainty & Desires)

The subjunctive is used for hypothetical situations, wishes, or uncertainty.

* **Present Subjunctive:** For regular verbs, "flip" the vowel pattern. If it is an **-AR** verb, use **-E** endings; if it is **-ER** or **-IR**, use **-A** endings.
* *Falar* → fale, fale, falemos, falem.
* *Comer* → coma, coma, comamos, comam.


* **Imperfect Subjunctive:** Used for "If I were..." scenarios. It is easily identified by the **-sse** sound.
* *Se eu falasse...* (If I spoke...)
* *Se eu tivesse...* (If I had...).



### Quick Tense Guide for Daily Speech

* **The Future Hack:** In everyday Brazilian Portuguese, the "proper" simple future (*Eu falarei*) is rarely used. Instead, use the present of the verb **ir** + the infinitive of the main verb (*Eu vou falar*).
* **Preterite vs. Imperfect:** Use the **Preterite** for actions that happened once and finished (*Eu comi* = I ate). Use the **Imperfect** for actions that used to happen or were ongoing (*Eu comia* = I was eating / I used to eat).
* **Imperative (Commands):** Brazilians often use the 3rd-person present indicative for informal commands (*Fala!* = Speak!). In formal settings or with specific verbs, they use the present subjunctive forms (*Fale!*).
